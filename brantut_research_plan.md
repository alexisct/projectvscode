## Research Workflow

### 1. Corpus Building

- Start from the GFZ profile and any linked publication sources
- Query Crossref for papers authored by Nicolas Brantut
- Collect DOI, title, year, journal, coauthors, abstract when available, and URL
- Merge duplicates and remove clearly irrelevant items

### 2. Relevance Screening

Papers will be tagged as:

- `core`: directly about fault stability, induced seismicity, friction, rupture, fluids, or modelling
- `secondary`: indirectly useful background on rock deformation, laboratory seismology, or constitutive behavior
- `peripheral`: outside the main scope for this review

### 3. Reading Strategy

Read in layers so the review stays efficient:

1. Full relevant set: titles, abstracts, conclusions
2. Core set: methods, figures, main results, discussion
3. Deep-dive subset: papers where experiments and models are explicitly coupled or iterated

### 4. Synthesis Strategy

The synthesis will focus on four threads:

- what Brantut argues about fault stability
- what his work implies for induced seismicity
- which modelling approaches he uses and how
- how experiments and models are used together to refine mechanism-level explanations
